World's Most Venomous Snake

In terms of potency, the most venomous snake in the world is the Inland Taipan. These snakes are native to Australia, and although their venom is highly toxic, they rarely come in contact with humans. In fact, the only reliable documented bite occurrences from the Inland Taipan have been with herpetologists who were handling them for research purposes.

How Roosters Protect Hearing

Few sounds are more loud and obnoxious than a crowing rooster, and apparently the roosters would agree with that sentiment, because they have a technique to protect their hearing from their own loud mouths. When roosters crow, they tilt their head back. This tilt covers their ear canals completely and serves as a sort of built-in ear plug.

Polar Bears Aren't Actually White

We've all been bamboozled by these brazen bears. Polar bear fur, although white in appearance, is actually transparent AND hollow. And, get this, their skin? It's black. Their black skin helps retain heat, as do the hollow hairs.

Longest Uninterrupted Flight

Alpine Swifts are a species of bird found in a lot of places, from southern Europe to the Middle East, and even parts of Asia. They also hold the world record for the longest recorded uninterrupted flight by a bird... over 200 days to be exact!

World's Largest Predator

Sperm Whales are the title holders for being the largest predator in the world. They can weigh in at over 80 tons and grow up to 20 meters in length! They're so large, they've been known to dive to great depths to feast on giant squid. You know, the squid with eyeballs the size of basketballs.

Corvids Are Super Intelligent

Corvids (crows, ravens, rooks, etc.) are highly intelligent birds, with many demonstrating the ability to mimic sounds and human voices, solve puzzles, recognize human faces, and even use tools. But did you know that some have even shown the ability to understand physics? Studies have shown that some of these birds have the same level of understanding physics as a 6 month old human infant, which is a higher level of comprehension than many apes.
